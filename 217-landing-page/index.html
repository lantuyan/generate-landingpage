HA-CUR-2024

The Learning Path

Five progressive stages from first mark to finished plate, each building the skills of observation and rendering.

I

Field Foundations

Learn to see before you draw. Field sketching techniques, botanical observation ethics, and the essentials of plant morphology.

  • 6 weeks guided study
  • Field notebook methods
  • Pencil & ink foundations
Beginner →
II

Form & Structure

Understanding plant architecture. Root systems, stem structures, leaf arrangements, and the geometry of botanical forms.

  • 8 weeks guided study
  • Scientific accuracy
  • Anatomical rendering
Foundation →
III

Watercolor Mastery

The transparent medium of botanical art. Layering washes, color mixing from limited palettes, and achieving luminosity.

  • 10 weeks guided study
  • Wet-on-wet techniques
  • Color theory for botanicals
Intermediate →
IV

Dissection & Detail

The scientific plate. Floral diagrams, cross-sections, and the precise rendering required for publication.

  • 8 weeks guided study
  • Magnification techniques
  • Publication standards
Advanced →
V

The Finished Plate

Creating exhibition-worthy work. Composition, scale, and the integration of habit and detail in a single plate.

  • 12 weeks guided study
  • Portfolio development
  • Exhibition preparation
Master →
