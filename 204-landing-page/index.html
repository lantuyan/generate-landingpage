4-7-8 Breathing

Developed by Dr. Andrew Weil, this technique acts as a "natural tranquilizer" for the nervous system. Inhale for 4, hold for 7, exhale for 8.

Sleep & Anxiety 2-3 min
02

Box Breathing

Used by Navy SEALs to maintain calm under pressure. Equal duration for inhale, hold, exhale, and hold creates rhythmic stability.

Panic Management 3-5 min
03

Coherent Breathing

Breathing at 5-6 breaths per minute optimizes heart rate variability and induces a state of calm alertness.

Daily Practice 5-10 min
04

Physiological Sigh

Two inhales through the nose, followed by a long exhale through the mouth. Rapidly offloads carbon dioxide and reduces stress.

The Science

Your Nervous System, Regulated

When anxiety strikes, your sympathetic nervous system activates—heart rate increases, breathing becomes shallow, muscles tense. Controlled breathing directly stimulates the vagus nerve, shifting your body into parasympathetic "rest and digest" mode.

StillPoint guides you through evidence-based protocols developed in clinical settings. Each exercise is calibrated to specific physiological outcomes, from reducing cortisol to improving heart rate variability.
